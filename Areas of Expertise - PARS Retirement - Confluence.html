Areas of Expertise
  • Created by Sharieff, Faiza (Contractor), last updated by Burnette, Elizabeth (Contractor) on Jun 15, 2021 4 minute read

1. Areas of Expertise - Internal screens 

  • The system shall allow the Program Staff to collect Areas of Expertise on the external response pages. 
    • Areas of Expertise will be free text fields

  • Current Workflow:

    • In Survey Monkey potential participants have both a populated list and free text field to send for panels. There are multiple kinds of lists, depending on the areas of expertise.
    • The working group noted that they would like to replicate that experience to have a selectable list and a free text field in MyNSF.
      • In survey money, the data is retained in an excel spreadsheet, when the panel is over.

  •  Future Workflow: 

    • The system shall display Areas of Expertise as an optional question that can be included in the participant request. 

    • The system shall allow the Areas of Expertise responses to be available to program staff via the response file download.

    • The system will allow staff to collect this information by creating their own list for potential reviewers to make selections in a checkbox type of selection AND/OR include an open text responses field,

    • Note: Based on interviews, the team proposed a maximum of 20 Areas of Expertise.

    • The system will automatically delete this information from the database after a pre-determined amount of time
      • Need to determine how long this information can be retained in the spreadsheet.
      • Need to determine if Areas of Expertise can be included on the Reviewer record in the MVP release.
    • The Areas of Expertise will only be available in the excel spreadsheet.
      • It will not populate in the reviewer record. It is retrievable in the future. But can only be accessed in the excel spreadsheet.
      • This information is being pulled from the availability request, not the reviewer record.
        • OGC raised a concern on PII with potential reviewers and storing Areas of Expertise. They stated that it is alright to use, but if it is being stored in association with the reviewer's name, this is a privacy act concern.
        • The system will not allow user to search for Areas of Expertise by name in the system.
          • The Areas of Expertise will be associated by request, not by name.


  • Action items based on feedback from the working group: 
    • Conduct analysis to determine when Areas of Expertise can be deleted from the excel sheet after a pre-determined length of time.
    • Plan to proceed with analysis to allow participants to select Areas of Expertise from a pre-populated list, as well as enter free text.
    • Determine a process for how to populate the selectable list.
      • Could there be a large list to select from and filter?
      • Suggestions were provided to filter by divisions and directorates 

2. Areas of Expertise - External screens

Future Workflow:

  • The potential participants will view the Areas of Expertise question that is included in Research.Gov, if Program Staff have selected the AOE question. 

  • The potential participants will view a maximum of 20 Areas of Expertise to choose from. 

  • The system shall allow additional Areas of Expertise to be added in the free text box. 

Development Approach - 

1

Can Areas of Expertise be deleted from the excel sheet after a pre-determined length of time? 

  • What will the process be for MyNSF? Is it deleted? Or is it incorporated in a reviewer record?

2

Will participants be able to select Areas of Expertise from a pre-populated list, as well as enter free text?


3

What process will be feasible for how to populate the selectable list?


2. Flexibility with the email template

  • Enhancements will be made to the Email Content tab:
  • Staff will have the ability to move the placement of the customized text section (Additional Information for Participants)
  • Additional static templates to send to potential Reviewers to include:
    • Reminder email
    • Thank you email 
    • Thank you, but we no longer need you 
    • Thank you for responding - Not an official email, but informs to "save the date" 
  • The team can reference eCorrespondence sent to Reviewers here: https://ej.acpt.nsf.gov/ej/showInlineEcorr.do?location=compose 
  • Text must be approved in advance by OGC
  • For consideration: Thank you template with options to include to meet the type of thank you letter.

Are there other templates or items that staff would like the team to consider for implementation?


  • A working group member mentioned 2 more types of emails: 
    • Thank you, but we no longer need you 
    • Thank you for responding - Not an official email, but informs to "save the date" 
Should there be a maximum number of templates?Team recommends no more than 4
Current frustrations with email template and what they need to convey to potential reviewers

Should there be one template for the thank you email, but allow for enough customization to allow for the different variations requested by the working group?

Does the working group need any additional enhancements like formatting, spell check, etc.?

Can the the team reuse the newly developed eJacket service to maintain these templates?Need to continue to coordinate with eJacket on timelines

How do we address flexibility with the customized text (additional Information for Participants) section in the current email template? Is this technically feasible to allow staff to move this section while keeping the rest of the template un-editable?

Need to highlight to working group that there are restrictions with templates 
Should the approach for the customized section be that the user has a choice of a few options where to place the text?

Should the current email template language be updated to include information such as dates for the panels

Variables have been an issue when displaying in the template. Should the team consider allowing staff to edit/customize those variables to make the email easier to read.
