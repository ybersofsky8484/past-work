    Usability Test Report - Reviewer Integration, Round 2
    • Created by Steen, Mindy (Contractor), last updated on Sep 27, 2022 8 minute read

    Overview

    The usability sessions focused on scenarios involving reviewers with no affiliations, entering and reviewing profile information and selecting disciplines. 

    • SESSIONS/USERS

      • Eight sessions occurred with users who have reviewed for NSF or participated in other meeting types.

        • How volunteer users were identified

          • By connections through Product Owner, Alex Wilson

          • Random selection from a database query of users who have reviewed in the past year

        • Users included Reviewers (8):

          • 8/8 have been on review panels

          • 5/8 have also done ad-hoc reviews
          • 7/8 have been or are currently also PIs
          • 2/8 have also participated in other meeting types
            • 2/8 Site Visit
            • 1/8 Advisory Committee, Committee of Visitors, Site Visit, Subcommittee, Workshop
      • Each session was approximately 45-60 minutes long during which participants completed three scenarios, including one with an alternate design. Task completion and satisfaction metrics were collected.


    High-level Findings

    • Overall, users did not feel burdened by providing their information, including home location, demographics, highest degree and discipline.

    I feel that it's necessary, so I don't feel it's burdensome at all."
    • Users that noticed that the Disciplines dropdown was filterable, loved the feature.
    I'm happy that when I started typing, it prepopulated with things..."

    • Most users struggled to find their highest degree.

    ...the Highest Degree menu - there seem to be replicated fields.

    * Four users were shown Version A first, then Version B, while the other four users were shown Version B first, then Version A

    Response to: "How satisfied were you with all the screens you saw today?"



    Design Options

    During the user feedback sessions - 

    • Two designs were shown for the Add Disciplines feature

    One, long list of Disciplines

    Separated list of Disciplines/Subdisciplines

    Users tended to prefer the one, long list of disciplines

    • Most users (5/8) preferred the single, long list of disciplines and subdisciplines
      • The 3/8 users who preferred the disciplines and subdisciplines to be broken down for selection, didn't realize that typing into the combobox to filter was an option. 

    "I like having everything available to me and not being limited by the pulldown."

    • Half the users (4/8) didn't realize they could add more than one discipline/subdiscipline
    • Half the users (4/8) didn't realize they were limited to five disciplines/subdisciplines

    Recommendation:

    MVP

    • Use the one, long list of disciplines/subdisciplines (Version A).
    • Consider showing five discipline fields by default (#2-5 being optional)

    Post-MVP

    • Collect metrics around how many disciplines/subdisciplines most users enter to see if we need to revisit the maximum of 5 entries.


    User Feedback

    Frequency of prompting users to ensure profile and affiliations are up-to-date

    • All users (8/8) indicated they rarely or never change their organizational affiliations

    "Not very often at all. I never change my job. I think for college professors, usually they don't change jobs."

    • None of the users (0/8) would mind being prompted to ensure their profile information and affiliations are up-to-date
      • 4/8 said once a year would be acceptable
      • 3/8 said once every two years would be acceptable
      • One user said they wouldn't mind if they were prompted in each session 

    "Once a year would be good. Affiliations and locations don't change that often, but expertise changes and areas of interest change."

    MVP

    • No changes

    Post-MVP

    • Once "Volunteer to Review" is in production, consider prompting users once or twice a year to review and update their reviewer profile.

    Jessica: Can we look at data - how much reviewers change institutions? Kristen will take a look. See if this data changes anything.

    Jessica: Prompt users once/year to review and update info (Post-MVP or with ad-hoc or volunteer to review)

    Some Disciplines were missing for users

    • Some users (5/8) could not find their discipline/subdiscipline in the given list, or noticed some disciplines/subdisciplines were missing.

    "It doesn't have the discipline I would usually select, biological engineering. You have bioengineering, but that's not the same thing."

    "Well, I don't see anything about education... Well, here's STEM education... but I wouldn't look over here. I guess I'd go to social sciences... I think this is odd having social sciences education under STEM education because it's not always STEM."

    Additional feedback: Reviewer Integration Usability Testing - Disciplines Feedback from Users

    MVP

    • Conduct unmoderated card sorting to get some quick feedback from users about how they would sort and label disciplines/subdisciplines in their field. 
      • NOTE: Requires OMB clearance
    • Monitor custom disciplines/subdisciplines that are entered for common entries that may need to be added.

    Post-MVP

    • Conduct usability testing again with the deployed design to validate that the functionality works well for users.

    Jessica: Card sort yes for MVP, assuming turnaround is reasonable. Prompt for disciplines/subdisciplines that are not included.

    Ask working group: Concerns if we receive one discipline for AoE?

    Update some language - plural, add language around what we use this for.

    Do additional testing with the implementation of ad-hoc reviews.

    Post-MVP: Plan on targeted satisfaction surveys for meeting participants.


    Findings and Recommendations

    Issue Severity Key

    HIGH  Prevents task completion, or causes extreme frustration for majority of users

    MEDIUM  Causes frustration or a delay in task completion, but task can eventually be completed

    LOW  Causes slight frustration or delay in task completion, or is more cosmetic in nature


    HIGH

    		1

    Not realizing the "combobox" (type-ahead dropdown feature) was also an entry field

    Challenge: 4 of 8 users struggled with finding their discipline and didn't realize they could type into the combobox feature.

    "Did you know you could type inside the menu?"
    "No. I would put... some indication that you can search within it."

    MVP

    • Include a search field below the dropdown when it is expanded. Focus in the field by default.

    Note: This is default behavior on the Research.gov sign-in screen and is also currently being developed in PSM.

    "The selection of the degree was kind of painful. I don't know that I would arrange things alphabetically; may go with abbreviations instead of spelled out."

    MVP

    • Update the list to include abbreviations, in alphabetical order.
    • Use the same design as disciplines/subdisciplines with the filter field feature since there are more than 15 items. (Excluding the "add other" feature.)

    • 5/8 users struggled with locating where to review or update their organizational affiliations outside of being a reviewer (PI, AOR, etc.)


    MVP

    • Update the left navigation with clearer grouping and terminology.

    Post-MVP

    • Consider options for consolidating reviewer organizational affiliations and other (non-reviewer) organizational affiliations. For example:

    Jessica: Analyze first to see if we can update navigation for MVP. Can be stretch candidate for post-MVP. Requires data call.

    MEDIUM

    		4

    Some users did not find My Profile to review their reviewer information entered

    • 2/8 users failed to find their profile to review their information entered during the reviewer integration process

    "Little messages that show up in the context of big information, I might not see."

    Note: This could be in-part because the wireframes are grayscale, which is lower contrast, making certain features such as the utility navigation in the top-right corner, more difficult to see.

    MVP

    Use the title "Update Reviewer Profile Information" for the integration process to make the connection between the information entered and the profile (see next item)

    Post-MVP

    Re-test with full-color screen to see if our assumptions are correct.

    		Jessica: Stretch for MVP. Lower item.

    LOW

    		5

    Title text of the reviewer integration process (Import/Update Reviewer Information) was problematic

    • 5/8 users said the process was clearly titled
    • 3/8 users indicated "Import" didn't make sense

    "I didn't see a place to import my info."

    MVP

    Use the title "Update Reviewer Profile Information" for the integration process

    Jessica: Send Jessica and Alex a few options for this title to use for MVP - Enter, Provide, etc.

    Keep in mind we need to change in the email.

    LOW

    		6

    Some users added a custom discipline without splitting it between discipline and subdiscipline

    • For example, two users (2/8) added "Music Psychology" in the Discipline field instead of "Psychology" in the Discipline field and "Music Psychology" in the Subdiscipline field.

    No changes here, but keep in mind that when disciplines/subdisciplines are entered, they may not be separated as you would hope.

    Jessica: We will present this result to working group (Wendy), bounce this off of Wendy's "person." After QP, present findings in a deck. 10/12.
