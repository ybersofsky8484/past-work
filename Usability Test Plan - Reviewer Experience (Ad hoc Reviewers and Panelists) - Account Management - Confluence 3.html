I. Usability Test Overview

Purpose 

This test is being conducted in response to feedback from the reviewer community, the desire to simplify the current complex process of signing into multiple systems to conduct different types of reviews. The usability sessions will focus on scenarios involving reviews, panels and other meeting types. We will be testing with users who have attended a meeting or submitted an ad hoc review in the past year.

Approver

The usability test plan needs to be approved by Jessica Turner prior to participants being scheduled. 

Target Users 

The sessions will include users who have attended a meeting or submitted an ad hoc review in the past year, and potentially users who are assigned to a meeting or an ad hoc review, but haven't submitted their review yet. This will include users who have submitted help desk tickets related to their reviewer experience.

Targeting 6-8 panelists/meeting participants or ad hoc reviewers for the study, including a subset of 3-4 users who have also submitted a related help desk ticket.

Users should have Research.gov accounts and prior experience with reviewing for NSF. 

Approach

A target number of 6-8 participants will be recruited and will participate in a 1 hour moderated test session with a User Experience (UX) Analyst. Sessions will be conducted remotely utilizing screen sharing. In these sessions, participants will be asked to complete key scenarios associated with reviews or meetings, and task completion and satisfaction metrics will be collected.  Upon completion of all test sessions and analysis of the data, the data will be compiled into a test report for the project team and client.

Session Format 

The usability test session adheres to the following format:

  1. The facilitator reads the orientation script to participants
  2. The facilitator asks participants any background questions
  3. Participants attempt to navigate through each scenario
    1. After each scenario, the facilitator asks the standard post-scenario questions, and subsequently any additional scenario follow-up questions
    2. Note: additional probing questions may occur throughout the scenarios as needed
  4. After all scenarios are completed, the facilitator asks the post-session questions

II. Usability Test Sessions 

Orientation Script 

The following will be read to participants at the start of the test session:

Thank you for participating in todays session. Just to provide some context, were conducting a usability study and collecting feedback on new Research.gov screens. The input we gather today will help us identify areas of the site that work well, and those that need improvement. In this session, we will:

  1. Present you with a few scenarios that ask you to locate information on wireframes of the site. We ask that you speak aloud as you navigate, telling us what's going through your mind as you decide where to go or what to click.

  2. Please know that this study is in no way a test of you, your skills, or your knowledge. It's a test of the system. If you feel frustrated at any point because you cant find something or dont know where to go, please let me know as these concerns should help identify areas of the site that need improvement.  However, since I'm here as a neutral observer, I won't be able to give you hints about where to go or what to click.

  3. The data from this study will be presented in aggregate form only, and your name will never be tied to your responses. Remember, I am a neutral observer, so you won't hurt my feelings by critiquing the site.  The most important thing about this study is that you're honest about what works for you and what doesn't, so we can fix it.

  4. Most of the wireframes you see today are created in greyscale to help keep your focus on the layout and functionality. Additionally, youll find that not every link or button may work, so well need to keep communicating throughout the session.


Any questions at this point?

Background Questions 

The following questions will be asked at the start of the session to better understand key criteria about the participants and/or track satisfaction of previously used systems. 

  1. Tell me about your current experience registering for or signing into your research.gov account.

  2. How long have you been a reviewer for NSF?

  3. Approximately, how many times have you participated as a reviewer for NSF?

  4. How many of those times was it a panel review?

  5. What other types of reviews have you been involved with for NSF?

  6. The last time you reviewed for NSF, how satisfied were you with the process?
    Very satisfied, Satisfied, Neutral, Dissatisfied, Very dissatisfied

Overview

Some of the screens you see today will include web forms that have been pre-populated with fake user data. On those screens, don't worry about filling in any information unless you are asked to provide something specific.

<Ask post-scenario questions>

7.1. If you got stuck during the process you just went through, what would you do next?

Post-Scenario Questions 

The following questions are asked after each scenario:

  1. How satisfied were you with the screens you just stepped through?
    Very satisfied, Satisfied, Neutral, Dissatisfied, Very dissatisfied
  2. What changes, if any, would you make to the screens?

Post-Session Questions 

The following questions are asked after all the scenarios have been completed, or as time allows:

  1. Overall, how satisfied are you with all the screens you just stepped through?
    Very satisfied, Satisfied, Neutral, Dissatisfied, Very dissatisfied
  2. Any additional comments or suggestions?

III. Usability Metrics 

A combination of both objective and subjective metrics is used to gauge the applications effectiveness, efficiency and level of user satisfaction.  The metrics that are collected during usability test sessions include:

  1. Scenario completion rate
    The completion rate is whether a participant has completed the scenario successfully without assistance from the facilitator. If assistance is required, or if the participant does not complete the scenario, it will be noted that the participant has failed to complete the scenario.    If the participant completes the scenario on their own, but has minor errors along the way and recovers, this is still treated as a success although it may be noted that difficulties occurred.  
  2. Post-scenario satisfaction rating
    After each scenario, regardless of whether the task was successfully completed or not, the user is asked for their rating on the satisfaction of the scenario.
  3. Post-session satisfaction rating
    After all the scenarios (or sooner if the participant doesnt have time to attempt all the scenarios in the test session), the user is asked for their rating on the satisfaction across all the scenarios.

If you are interested in participating, please register for an open session. Once registered, you will immediately be sent a calendar invitation with further session details.
