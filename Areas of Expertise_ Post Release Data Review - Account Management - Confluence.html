Proposed New Area of Expertise (AOE) Menu Items

Based on User-generated "Other" Area of Expertise Entries

Of 2,357 total user-generated "Other" entries, 277 were entered by more than one user. 

Questions:

  • Is 2 instances an acceptable threshold for adding a new menu item, or should it be higher?
  • Evaluated on a case-by-case basis?

Observations

Unhyphenated "Parent" Categories 

In a few instances, users entered top-level categories for terms that are hyphenated in the Areas of Expertise dropdown menu list. 

Data Science

1

Electrical Engineering

1

Machine Learning

1

Mathematics

2

Physics

1

Project Management

2

Typos

Some users misspelled words. Assuming no action is required on our part. Examples:

  • Adminstration
  • Celular Therapy

Possible Future Enhancement: Main Menu Duplicate Handling

The following user-generated AOEs are duplicates of AOEs in the dropdown menu. The existing AOE component prevents users from entering a duplicate of a term they have already entered, but it does not compare users' text entries to the dropdown menu list.

  • Clinical Psychology
  • Comparative Biology
  • Education
  • Education Technology
  • Engineering Education
  • Evolutionary Ecology
  • Higher Education
  • International Relations
  • Mathematical Biology
  • Optical Engineering
  • Public Policy

The code change to compare user entries to the main menu would not be a quick fix and only 0.47% of custom entries are duplicates.
Would it make sense to continue monitoring the data and check to see if that percentage has increased significantly in another six months?
